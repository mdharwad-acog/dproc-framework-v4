üìã Executive Summary

CRISPR-Cas9 gene editing has emerged as a revolutionary and highly efficient tool, fundamentally transforming the landscape of genetic research and therapeutic development. Initially recognized for its speed, ease, and cost-effectiveness compared to earlier methods like Zinc Finger Nucleases (ZFN) and Transcription-Activator Like Effector Nucleases (TALEN), the system enables precise targeted genome modification across diverse organisms, including animals, plants, and protozoan parasites. Its versatility extends to applications such as gene knock-out, regulation of endogenous gene expression, live-cell chromosomal labeling, RNA editing, and high-throughput gene screening, significantly advancing the generation of disease models and the understanding of gene function.
